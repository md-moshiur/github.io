Carburizing Process

Thermochemical surface treatment that enriches the surface with carbon to enable deep case hardening through subsequent heat treatment

What is Carburizing?

Carburizing is a thermochemical process where carbon is diffused into the surface of low-carbon steel at high temperatures. This creates a high-carbon surface layer that can be subsequently hardened through quenching, while maintaining a tough, low-carbon core.

Temperature Range

900-950°C (austenitic range)

Atmosphere

CO, CH₄, or solid carbon compounds

Process Time

4-24 hours depending on case depth

Industrial Applications

Carburizing is essential for components requiring hard, wear-resistant surfaces with tough cores

Gears

Automotive and industrial gears requiring wear resistance and fatigue strength

Automotive

Camshafts, crankshafts, and transmission components

Machine Tools

Cutting tools, dies, and precision machine components

Heavy Industry

Bearings, shafts, and high-load mechanical components
