Quenching Process

Rapid cooling heat treatment that transforms austenite to martensite, achieving maximum hardness in steel

What is Quenching?

Quenching is a rapid cooling process where steel is heated to the austenitic temperature range and then cooled quickly using water, oil, or other quenchants. This rapid cooling prevents the formation of pearlite and bainite, instead forming martensite - a hard, brittle phase.

Quenching Media

Different quenching media provide varying cooling rates and are selected based on steel composition and desired properties

Water

Fastest cooling rate, highest hardness but increased risk of cracking and distortion.

Cooling Rate: Very Fast
Hardness: Maximum
Distortion: High

Oil

Moderate cooling rate with reduced cracking risk, suitable for most applications.

Cooling Rate: Moderate
Hardness: High
Distortion: Medium

Polymer

Controllable cooling rate through concentration adjustment, good uniformity.

Cooling Rate: Adjustable
Hardness: Variable
Distortion: Low

Gas

Slowest cooling rate, minimal distortion, suitable for complex geometries.

Cooling Rate: Slow
Hardness: Moderate
Distortion: Minimal
