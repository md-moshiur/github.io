Annealing Process

Softening heat treatment that reduces hardness, relieves internal stresses, and improves ductility through controlled heating and slow cooling

What is Annealing?

Annealing is a heat treatment process that involves heating steel to a specific temperature, holding it at that temperature for a predetermined time, and then cooling it slowly (usually in the furnace). This process is designed to soften the material, relieve internal stresses, and improve machinability.

Temperature Range

Typically 700-900°C depending on steel composition

Holding Time

1-8 hours depending on section thickness

Cooling Rate

Slow furnace cooling (10-50°C/hour)

Types of Annealing

Different annealing processes are used depending on the desired outcome and steel composition

Full Annealing

Complete softening treatment heating above the upper critical temperature (Ac3) followed by slow cooling.

Temperature: Ac3 + 30-50°C
Microstructure: Pearlite + Ferrite
Application: Maximum softness

Stress Relief

Low-temperature treatment to relieve internal stresses without significant microstructural changes.

Temperature: 550-650°C
Microstructure: Unchanged
Application: Stress relief

Spheroidizing

Process to produce spheroidal carbides for improved machinability in high-carbon steels.

Temperature: 700-750°C
Microstructure: Spheroidal carbides
Application: Machinability

Process Annealing

Intermediate annealing during cold working to restore ductility for continued deformation.

Temperature: 650-700°C
Microstructure: Recrystallized
Application: Cold working

Normalizing

Air cooling from austenitic temperature to refine grain structure and improve uniformity.

Temperature: Ac3 + 50°C
Microstructure: Fine pearlite
Application: Grain refinement

Subcritical

Annealing below the critical temperature to reduce hardness without phase transformation.

Temperature: Below Ac1
Microstructure: Tempered
Application: Partial softening

Microstructural Changes

Understanding the microstructural transformations during annealing

Phase Transformations

Heating Phase

Pearlite → Austenite transformation above Ac1

Soaking Phase

Homogenization of austenite composition

Cooling Phase

Austenite → Coarse pearlite + ferrite

Grain Structure Changes

  • Grain growth during heating
  • Recrystallization of deformed grains
  • Formation of equiaxed grain structure
  • Coarse pearlite formation during cooling

Microstructure Control

Achieving desired grain structure and uniformity
