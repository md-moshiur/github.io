Understanding Heat Treatment

Heat treatment is a controlled process of heating and cooling metals to alter their physical and mechanical properties without changing their shape.

Temperature Control

Precise heating to specific temperatures to achieve desired microstructural changes

Time Management

Controlled holding times to allow complete phase transformations

Cooling Rate

Strategic cooling methods to achieve target mechanical properties

Heat Treatment Processes

Explore the fundamental heat treatment processes used in metallurgical engineering

Annealing

Softening treatment that reduces hardness and increases ductility through controlled heating and slow cooling.

Temperature: 700-900°C
Cooling: Furnace Cool
Purpose: Stress Relief
Learn More

Quenching

Rapid cooling process that hardens steel by forming martensite through fast heat extraction.

Temperature: 850-950°C
Cooling: Water/Oil
Purpose: Hardening
Learn More

Tempering

Secondary treatment that reduces brittleness while maintaining hardness through controlled reheating.

Temperature: 150-650°C
Cooling: Air Cool
Purpose: Toughening
Learn More

Nitriding

Surface hardening process that introduces nitrogen to form hard nitride compounds in the surface layer.

Temperature: 500-580°C
Atmosphere: Ammonia
Purpose: Surface Hard
Learn More

Carburizing

Carbon enrichment process that increases surface carbon content for subsequent hardening treatments.

Temperature: 900-950°C
Atmosphere: CO/CH4
Purpose: Case Harden
Learn More

Surface Treatments

Advanced surface modification techniques including plasma treatments and thermochemical processes.
